About Me

I am a mathematician working at the intersection of Calculus of Variations, geometric measure theory, and partial differential equations.

My research focuses on existence, regularity, and qualitative properties of minimizers for variational integrals of the form \[ \mathcal{F}[u] = \int_{\Omega} F\!\left(x,\, u(x),\, \nabla u(x)\right) dx, \] where \(F\) satisfies general growth and coercivity conditions. I am particularly interested in cases where the integrand \(F(\xi)\) is quasiconvex but not polyconvex, leading to subtle relaxation phenomena.

Before joining [University], I held postdoctoral positions at [Institution A] and [Institution B]. I completed my PhD at [PhD Institution] under the supervision of [Supervisor Name].

A Favourite Result

Theorem (Morrey, 1952)

A continuous function \(F\colon \mathbb{R}^{m\times n}\to\mathbb{R}\) is quasiconvex if and only if for every open bounded \(\Omega\subset\mathbb{R}^n\) and every \(\varphi\in W^{1,\infty}_0(\Omega;\mathbb{R}^m)\), \[ F(A)\,|\Omega| \;\leq\; \int_\Omega F\!\left(A + \nabla\varphi(x)\right)dx. \]
